Netflix vs. Blockbuster: The Classic Disruption Tale

Think back to 2004. Blockbuster was a giant with thousands of stores. People loved their Friday night movie rituals — driving to the store, browsing the aisles, picking out a DVD, and maybe grabbing some overpriced popcorn. Then came Netflix. Initially, they seemed like a cute little startup with an odd idea of renting DVDs by mail. Blockbuster could have bought Netflix for a measly $50 million, but they passed.

 

Fast forward to today, and Netflix is worth over $180 billion, while Blockbuster went bankrupt in 2010. What happened? Blockbuster clung to the old way of doing things, refusing to adapt and embrace new tech. Netflix, on the other hand, saw the future, invested in it, and positioned itself as the leader of the streaming revolution.
