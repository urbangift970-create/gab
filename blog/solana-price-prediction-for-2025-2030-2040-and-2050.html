Solana Explained: What Makes It Special?

Solana's blockchain platform was created to handle cryptocurrency transactions and make developing decentralized apps or dApps easier. Solana is distinguished by its proprietary timestamp technology and hybrid protocol system, built around the Proof-of-History (PoH) method. 

 

The verifiable and secure timestamp that this system generates for each transaction enables nodes to perform transactions without requiring communication from one another, greatly reducing the processing time of transactions. 

 

PoH functions in concert with a Proof-of-Stake (PoS) consensus mechanism in Solana's hybrid model. By capturing the sequence of events and the time interval between them, PoH reduces the necessity for conventional consensus-building procedures. 

 

The Proof of Stake (PoS) process completes these transactions and safeguards the network by letting validators who have an interest in the system suggest and decide whether or not to approve additional blocks.

Solana is an inflationary crypto with no fixed cap on the total number of tokens. The coin started with an annual inflation rate of 8%, which will decrease by 15% each year until it reaches 1.5%, which will stay fixed for the long term. 

 

At the start, 37% of the SOL tokens were distributed to investors, and 25% were split between the Solana team and the Solana Foundation, a nonprofit company exploring crypto. Meanwhile, 38% were reserved for the Solana Foundation's community fund. 

 

The SOL was launched in March 2020, and the price of SOL hovered around $0.50 and $1.50 for the following year. Then, in the crypto bull run of 2021, the coin became a top-performing asset as its price hiked to $55.91 in May 2021.

Difference Between Solana And Other Blockchains?

Solana blockchain ranks among the top blockchains when it comes to security, scalability and performance. Let’s see some of the main factors that make Solana the best blockchain. 

 

1. Proof of History (PoH): 

 

Unlike mechanisms like Proof of Stake (PoS) or Proof of Work (PoW), Solana uses its unique system, Proof of History. The architecture enhances the coin's speed and efficiency by keeping track of all past transactions. 

 

2. Scalability and Performance: 

 

Solana has the potential to manage 65,000 transactions per second, which is far greater than the speed of many other blockchains. The speed originates due to SOL's unique structure, PoH mechanism, and the adoption of pipelining processes and concurrent processing.

 

3. Hybrid Model: 

 

Solana uses a hybrid consensus model that fuses the PoS and PoH mechanisms. PoS provides enhanced security, while PoH gives the system a decentralized, unique clock. 

 

4. High-Tech Technology: 

 

Solana uses multiple highly advanced technologies, such as parallel and GPU. These factors help SOL process smart contracts and transactions more quickly and efficiently.
